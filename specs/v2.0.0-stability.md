# v2.0.0 Stability — Tracking Doc

**Status**: Draft (2026-05-08)
**Owns**: the running list of stable-surface changes whose breaking
nature requires a v2.0.0 major bump, the v1.x deprecation steps that
must precede each one, and the unresolved open questions inherited
from v1.0.0-stability.md §7 that may shape v2.0.
**Supersedes / cross-references**: `specs/v1.0.0-stability.md` is the
canonical statement of what stable means. This doc does not redefine
that — it enumerates v2.0-candidate breaks against the v1.0.0 surface.
For purely internal-tier cleanup (no semver impact), see CHANGELOG
"Removed (internal-not-stable surface)" entries instead.

This is a tracker spec, not a manifesto. The purpose is to make every
"queued for v2.0" mention scattered across `src/`, `specs/`, `docs/`,
and `CHANGELOG.md` legible in one place, with the constitutional /
stability rationale that blocks each item from landing earlier.
**Prioritization happens later.** Adding an entry here does not commit
v2.0 to landing it.

---

## 1. Versioning policy (carried forward unchanged)

The semver and deprecation rules defined in `specs/v1.0.0-stability.md`
§5–§6 remain in force from v1.0.0 onward — major bumps are required
for any break to the §1 stable surface, and at least one minor release
with `DeprecationWarning` plus a CHANGELOG `Migration` recipe must
precede each removal. Constitution Chapter VI ratifies this as a
binding evolution promise.

This spec changes neither §5 nor §6. v2.0.0 is governed by the same
rules v1.0.0 ratified. Any future revision to those rules requires its
own constitutional amendment.

---

## 2. Stable surface (carried forward unchanged from v1.0.0 §1)

The §1 enumeration in `specs/v1.0.0-stability.md` defines the stable
public surface for the v1.x line and continues into v2.x except for
the explicit breaks listed in §3. v2.0.0 does not redefine the shape
of "stable"; it only narrows / replaces specific names.

---

## 3. Candidate v2.0 breaks

Each entry records: what is queued, why it is a major break (which
stability or constitutional promise blocks doing it earlier in v1.x),
what the v1.x deprecation step looks like per `v1.0.0-stability.md`
§6, and whether that deprecation has started.

### 3.1 `TraceEvent.role: AgentRole` → `agent_name: str | None`

**What goes**: the `role: AgentRole` field on
`arcana.contracts.trace.TraceEvent`, plus the entire
`arcana.contracts.trace.AgentRole` enum (`SYSTEM`, `PLANNER`,
`EXECUTOR`, `CRITIC`).

**What replaces it**: a free-form `agent_name: str | None` field on
`TraceEvent`. Multi-agent attribution is by user-chosen agent name
(the same name the agent is registered under in `pool.add(name=...)`),
not by a framework-prescribed role enum.

**Why a major break**: `TraceEvent` is part of the v1.0.0 stable
contract surface (`arcana.contracts.trace`, listed in
`v1.0.0-stability.md` §1.4). Its `role` field is typed as `AgentRole`,
and per §5 of that spec, "field rename = major bump, field addition
with default = minor bump." Replacing the field's type with a free-form
string is a rename in semver terms — an outright behavior change for
any consumer that pattern-matches on `event.role == AgentRole.PLANNER`
or relies on the four-element domain. There is no constitutional way
to soft-land it inside the v1.x line.

**Why constitutionally queued**: Constitution Amendment 3 (v3.4,
2026-05-03) makes Principle 8's "framework never decides topology"
binding for multi-agent sessions. `AgentRole`'s
`PLANNER`/`EXECUTOR`/`CRITIC` members encoded a framework-prescribed
Planner→Executor→Critic topology that the amendment rules out. The
enum survived v1.0.0 only because its consumers (`TeamOrchestrator`,
`MessageBus`) were removed in `arcana.multi_agent.*` (internal-tier,
zero-window deprecation per CHANGELOG `[Unreleased]`), while the
*type of `TraceEvent.role`* sits on a stable name and could not be
changed in the same release without a major bump.

**v1.x deprecation step (per §6 of v1.0.0-stability)**:

A pre-v2.0 minor must:
- Add a runtime `DeprecationWarning` whenever `TraceEvent.role` is
  read or written with a non-`SYSTEM` value, OR introduce
  `agent_name: str | None` as the new field with `role` aliased to
  it for backward compatibility, OR both.
- Ship the new `agent_name`-keyed APIs in the same release where the
  deprecation lands — the §6 "no use the future API once we build it"
  rule applies.
- Include a CHANGELOG `Migration` section with before/after snippets.

**Has the deprecation started?** Partially. The vestigial docstring
on the `AgentRole` enum (landed in commit `7603a4b`, 2026-05-03,
Amendment 3 step 3) announces the future change but does not emit
`DeprecationWarning` and does not introduce the replacement field.
Per a strict reading of §6 ("minimum one minor release with
`DeprecationWarning`"), the deprecation cycle has NOT formally begun.
A v1.x minor release that lands the `DeprecationWarning` plus the
`agent_name` field is required before v2.0 may remove the old shape.

**Tracking pointers**:
- `src/arcana/contracts/trace.py:27` (the enum, with vestigial
  docstring at `:30-41`)
- `src/arcana/contracts/trace.py:190` (`TraceEvent.role` field)
- `specs/constitution-amendment-3-multi-agent-os.md:131` ("Step 4
  (v2.0 work)")
- `docs/guide/multi-agent.md:382-390`
- `CHANGELOG.md` `[Unreleased]` line 19

### 3.2 `StreamEventType` inert enum members — `TEXT_DELTA` and `THINKING`

**What goes (candidate)**: the two inert members
`StreamEventType.TEXT_DELTA` and `StreamEventType.THINKING` on
`arcana.contracts.streaming.StreamEventType`. Both were introduced
in v0.3.0's "structured agent events" group, never wired into the
emit path, never tested, never documented.

**Why a major break**: `StreamEventType` is part of
`arcana.contracts.streaming`, listed in v1.0.0-stability.md §1.4.
v1.0.0-stability.md §4.2 explicitly observes: "removing public enum
members is a major break, so removal would need its own deprecation
cycle (warn in a future minor, remove in v2.0.0)." The motivation
for that cycle did not exist for v1.0.0 (the inert members cost
nothing); whether it exists for v2.0 is a separate decision.

**Why constitutionally queued**: this is *not* a constitutional
violation. The members are dead code, not principled-wrong code.
Inclusion here is purely a semver consequence — if v2.0 chooses to
remove them, this is the gate.

**v1.x deprecation step (per §6 of v1.0.0-stability)**:
- Add `DeprecationWarning` on construction or comparison of the two
  members in a v1.x minor.
- Document in CHANGELOG with `Migration` (no migration needed; the
  members were never emitted, so no user code can depend on receiving
  them — only on referencing them, which is uncommon).

**Has the deprecation started?** No. v1.0.0-stability.md §4.2 was
explicitly closed as a no-op. This entry exists only so the option
is legible if v2.0 wants to act on it.

**Tracking pointers**:
- `src/arcana/contracts/streaming.py:32-33`
- `specs/v1.0.0-stability.md:309-338` (§4.2 closed-as-no-op rationale)

## 4. Long-term roadmap items (not concretely queued for v2.0)

These have been mentioned in passing as eventual changes but have no
v1.x deprecation in flight and no spec-level argument for landing in
v2.0 specifically. Listed for completeness so future planning can
locate them quickly.

### 4.1 V1 engine (`engine="adaptive"`, `Agent` + `AdaptivePolicy` + `Reducer`)

`docs/architecture.md` §10 names V1 as "长期路线图：当 V1 用户全部迁移到
V2 后移除" — long-term roadmap, removal once V1 users have all migrated.
`specs/v1.0.0-removals.md` "Not scheduled for removal" entry confirms:
"separate question, not part of this removal doc. If it is ever to be
removed, that needs its own entry."

The `engine="adaptive"` parameter on `arcana.run()` and `Runtime.run()`
sits on the v1.0.0 stable surface (it's a documented signature of
public methods), so removal would be a major break. Not deprecated;
not concretely queued for v2.0; included here so the user can decide
whether to start a deprecation cycle in a v1.x minor if v2.0 should
remove it.

### 4.2 `RuntimeConfig` field-set scope

Open question carried forward from `v1.0.0-stability.md` §7: "Is
`RuntimeConfig` itself part of the stable contract, or only the field
set we currently document? (Lean: the public field set; new fields
are minor bumps; renamed/removed fields are major.)"

This is a clarification, not a break. v2.0 is the natural moment to
either ratify the lean or restate it explicitly in §1. No code change
is implied; what changes is the doc commitment.

## 6. Adding new entries

Any new "queued for v2.0" decision must add an entry to §3 with:
- What goes / what replaces it.
- Why it is a major break (cite the v1.0.0 §1 surface entry).
- Why it cannot land in a v1.x minor (constitutional or
  stability-promise rationale).
- The v1.x deprecation plan per `v1.0.0-stability.md` §6.
- Whether that deprecation has started, and if so where the
  `DeprecationWarning` lives.

If the surface in question is in §2 of v1.0.0-stability.md
(internal-not-stable tier), the change does not need to wait for
v2.0 — it can land as a CHANGELOG "Removed (internal-not-stable
surface)" entry in any release. Do not add internal-tier cleanup
here.
